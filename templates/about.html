About Me

This is what I do.

I’m Akshat Mehta — a coder, writer, thinker, and warrior in the making.

Parabellum Diaries is my digital battlefield — where thoughts, ideas, reflections, and hard truths collide. It’s where I document not just code and creativity, but conflict — inner, outer, and societal.

The name isn’t just a fancy Latin phrase. “Si vis pacem, para bellum” — If you want peace, prepare for war — reminds me that life rewards those who are ready. Ready to think. Ready to act. Ready to rebuild.

I’m a Computer Science student. But beyond the curriculum, I explore technology, philosophy, psychology, space, and the edges of human potential. I believe that writing is not just self-expression — it’s self-definition.

Whether it's a deep dive into code, a thought on consciousness, a rant about society, or a poem written in battle-mode — this is where it all lands.

Welcome to the war journal. This is Parabellum Diaries.
